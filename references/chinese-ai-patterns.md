# Chinese Academic AI-Writing Pattern Checklist

Use this checklist when revising Chinese academic prose. It focuses on Chinese thesis, journal paper, project report, and translated-paper expressions that often sound generated, formulaic, or administratively inflated. Preserve factual content, technical terms, citations, and claim strength.

## Opening and Background Templates

1. "随着...的发展" opening: revise only when it is a generic setup rather than necessary context.
2. "近年来...受到广泛关注" without specifying why the paper needs this context.
3. "在新时代/新形势/新背景下" when it sounds like policy or report language rather than academic argument.
4. "面对...挑战" followed by no concrete challenge tied to the study.
5. "如何...成为亟待解决的问题" when the paper does not actually establish urgency.

## Research Purpose and Contribution Templates

6. "本文旨在..." repeated as a default objective marker. Prefer "本文考察/分析/构建/评估..." when possible.
7. "具有重要理论意义和实践价值" without a specific contribution.
8. "为...提供参考/借鉴/支撑" as an empty ending.
9. "填补了...空白" when the source only narrows or extends prior work.
10. "实现了...创新" without identifying the exact methodological or empirical change.

## Empty Evaluation and Overclaiming

11. "显著提升/有效提升" without numerical evidence or comparison baseline.
12. "充分证明/有力证明" when results only "表明/显示/支持".
13. "具有较强鲁棒性/泛化能力" without stress tests, external validation, or cross-domain evidence.
14. "全面/系统/深入" used as decoration rather than describing actual scope.
15. "科学性、合理性、有效性" listed together without separate evidence.

## Chinese Logical Connectors

16. Repeated "首先、其次、最后" when the paragraph is not a true sequence.
17. Repeated "此外、进一步、与此同时" when the relation is just additive.
18. "因此/由此可见" before a conclusion that does not follow from the previous sentence.
19. "从...角度来看/从...层面出发" used to create fake structure.
20. "一方面...另一方面..." where the two sides are not balanced or contrastive.

## Noun-Heavy and Administrative Style

21. Long abstract noun chains: "对...的提升的实现", "基于...的...机制构建".
22. "体系/机制/路径/框架/模式" used when the work only proposes a method, model, or procedure.
23. "赋能/驱动/协同/闭环/多维度" when borrowed from policy, management, or product language.
24. "以...为抓手/以...为导向/以...为核心" in ordinary academic prose.
25. Nominalization that hides the actual actor, method, or result.

## Machine-Translation and Mixed-Language Issues

26. English structure mapped directly into Chinese, e.g. "该方法被用来去..." when a simpler active sentence is natural.
27. Redundant bilingual terms after the term has already been defined.
28. Inconsistent translation of the same technical term.
29. Awkward "的" chains caused by translated noun modifiers.
30. Overuse of "其" where a clearer noun or pronoun is needed.

## Results, Methods, and Discussion

31. Method sections using promotional language instead of reproducible procedure.
32. Results sections mixing observation and interpretation without separation.
33. "实验结果表明..." used repeatedly at the start of adjacent sentences.
34. Discussion sections adding implications not supported by the reported results.
35. Conclusion sections expanding into unsupported future prospects or field-level claims.
36. Limitations omitted or softened when the original text contains scope constraints.

## Revision Rule

When a pattern appears, replace it with a more specific academic formulation only if the source provides enough information. If the source lacks evidence for specificity, keep the claim cautious and note the limitation in the comparison table.
