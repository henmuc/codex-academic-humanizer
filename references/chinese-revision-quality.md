# Chinese Academic Revision Quality

Use this reference after revising Chinese academic prose. It adapts the useful "quick checklist" and quality-scoring ideas from general Chinese humanizer workflows to paper editing. The goal is not to make the text casual; the goal is to make it read like a careful academic author wrote it.

## Quick Final Checklist

Before delivering the revision, check:

- Are three adjacent sentences built with nearly the same rhythm or length? Vary one sentence if doing so does not change meaning.
- Does the paragraph end with a generic phrase such as "提供参考", "奠定基础", or "具有重要意义"? Replace it with a concrete, source-supported statement or remove it.
- Are "此外", "进一步", "与此同时", "因此" used as automatic connectors? Keep only connectors that express a real relation.
- Are there three-item lists that only exist to sound comprehensive? Use the actual number of source-supported items.
- Are there decorative abstract terms such as "赋能", "闭环", "体系化", "多维度", or "路径机制"? Keep them only when they name a real construct in the paper.
- Are method and result sentences still reproducible and evidence-based after rewriting?
- Are citations, variables, units, figure/table references, formulas, and section labels unchanged?
- Is the claim strength still aligned with the evidence?

## Academic Human Voice

For papers, "human voice" means disciplined authorial judgment, not personal tone.

Prefer:

- Concrete research objects over broad field slogans.
- Measured claims over promotional conclusions.
- Clear logical relations over decorative transitions.
- Stable terminology over synonym cycling.
- Specific limitations or scope conditions over vague optimism.

Avoid:

- Adding "I/we feel" unless the manuscript already uses first-person academic voice.
- Adding humor, emotion, anecdote, or rhetorical flourish.
- Making the text conversational when it should remain formal.

## Quality Rubric

Use this rubric internally. Report scores only when the user asks for evaluation.

| Dimension | Good Revision | Risk Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Directness | States the research action or finding directly | Opens with broad slogans or padded background |
| Evidence alignment | Claim strength matches data, citation, and method | Uses "显著", "充分证明", or causal wording without support |
| Rhythm | Sentence length and structure vary naturally | Sentences are uniformly balanced and over-clean |
| Terminology | Key terms are consistent | Same construct is renamed repeatedly |
| Academic register | Formal, precise, and field-appropriate | Blog-like, promotional, administrative, or policy-report tone |
| Preservation | Facts, citations, variables, and references are intact | Adds facts, removes hedging, or changes technical scope |

## When to Flag Instead of Rewrite

Flag the issue in the comparison notes when:

- The original claim is unsupported but cannot be made specific from the given text.
- A term appears to have multiple possible translations.
- A citation's scope is unclear.
- A sentence appears to need domain knowledge not present in the input.
